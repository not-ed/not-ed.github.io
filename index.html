About Me

I am a Software Developer based in the United Kingdom with a mixture of self-taught and formal programming education and knowledge. Having recently made the choice to switch career paths into Tech, I bring with me a background in Games Programming, including brief experience in-industry as a Tester. With a love for computers and technology, a commitment to life-long learning and a drive to perform at the highest level I possibly can, I am currently pursuing a Bachelor’s in Computer Science at Staffordshire University in order to take advantage of a guided learning path and obtain a recognized qualification within the field, all while continuing to explore interests and projects of my own on the side.

Work

When it comes to the projects I end up working on, finishing them often come with so many stories to tell about how it was made, both for better and worse in regard to how they were carried out. It could be about some new concept that I’ve decided to explore, what struggles had to be overcome in order to get the job done, or just about why I decided to tape a Raspberry Pi to a wall. Game Work

Like a lot of others, I actually ended up getting my start in programming because I wanted to learn how to make my own games when I was a kid. I originally cut my teeth playing around with the odd game-making tool here and there when I was younger, but once I was introduced to Object-Oriented Programming in college, it opened up a much wider range of possibilities to me, and it all became downhill from there. While I have since chosen to move on from the Games Industry in a professional capacity over into Tech, there is nothing still stopping me from creating something here and there if I ever feel like it, and like the rest of my other projects, I feel that each game made still has lasting value in the challenges I had to overcome to make them.
